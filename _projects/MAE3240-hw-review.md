This is the third question of problem set 3 for my class MAE3240: Heat Transfer.

Working through this problem helped me better understand how thermal resistance networks can be used to analyze complex heat transfer problems in real engineering systems. By modeling the turbine blade as a series of thermal resistances, including interfacial thermal resistance, convection, and conduction-based resistances, I further developed my understanding of how different mechanisms of heat transfer interact within a single system. Comparing cases with and without the thermal barrier coating emphasized how material properties, dimensions, and interfacial effects influence the temperature distribution. This problem reinforced the idea that relatively thin layers with low thermal conductivity can drastically reduce heat transfer and protect structural components from extreme environments.

This concept is especially relevant to my interests in aerospace engineering. Turbine blades in jet engines and rocket propulsion systems operate at extremely high temperatures, making heat management critical. This problem helped me appreciate how fundamental heat transfer analysis directly supports the design of advanced mechanical and aerospace systems, and it strengthened skills that will be valuable as I move forward in my academic career and beyond.
